FOOD CULTURE

INDIAN FOOD

  1. Indian cuisine consists of a variety of regional and traditional cuisines native to the Indian subcontinent
  2. Indian food is also heavily influenced by religion, in particular Hinduism, cultural choices and traditions.
  3. Centuries of Islamic rule, particularly by the Mughals, also introduced dishes like samosa and pilaf.
  4. Early diet in India mainly consisted of legumes, vegetables, fruits, grains, dairy products, and honey.

CHINEASE FOOD

  1. A Chinese meal is consisted of two parts: staple food, normally made of rice, noodles or steamed buns, and ts'ai, vegetable and meat dishes
  2. The primary eating utensils are chopsticks (for solid foods) and ceramic spoon (for soups and congees).
  3. In a Chinese meal, everyone will have their own rice bowl; however, the accompanying dishes will be served in communal plates and shared by all people.

ITALIAN FOOD

  1. It influenced several cuisines around the world, chiefly that of the United States.
  2. Italian cuisine is generally characterized by its simplicity, with many dishes having only two to four main ingredients.
  3. Italian cooks rely chiefly on the quality of the ingredients rather than on elaborate preparation.
  4. Many dishes that were once regional have proliferated with variations throughout the country.
